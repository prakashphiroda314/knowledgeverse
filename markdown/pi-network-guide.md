Pi Network has captured the imagination of over 60 million users worldwide. It promises something unique in the world of cryptocurrency: a digital currency you can mine from your smartphone, without draining your battery or requiring expensive hardware. But what exactly is Pi Network? Is it legitimate? And what does the future hold?

This comprehensive guide answers every question you have — from the technical mechanics to investment considerations to the road ahead.

## What Is Pi Network?

Pi Network is a cryptocurrency project founded in 2019 by Dr. Nicolas Kokkalis and Dr. Chengdiao Fan, both Stanford PhD graduates. Their vision was radical: create a digital currency that ordinary people could mine using just their smartphones, making cryptocurrency accessible to everyone — not just those with expensive mining rigs or technical expertise.

:::info
**Key Fact:** Pi Network uses the Stellar Consensus Protocol (SCP), not the energy-intensive Proof of Work used by Bitcoin. This is why Pi can be mined without draining your phone's battery.
:::

Pi is built on a layered trust model: you mine Pi by being an active member of the network and vouching for others in your security circle. The more trustworthy your network, the more Pi you mine.

## How Pi Network Works

### The Stellar Consensus Protocol (SCP)

Unlike Bitcoin, which requires miners to solve complex mathematical puzzles using massive computing power, Pi uses the Stellar Consensus Protocol. SCP achieves consensus through a web of trusted connections between nodes rather than computational brute force.

This is why mining Pi costs virtually no battery power — your phone isn't solving mathematical puzzles. It's simply confirming your active participation in the network and contributing to consensus.

### Security Circles

Your security circle is a group of 3–5 people you trust. When you vouch for someone, you're saying you believe they are a real, trustworthy person. This creates a web of human trust that validates transactions on the network.

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| Pioneer | Basic miner — opens the app daily to confirm humanity |
| Contributor | Builds a security circle of 3–5 trusted members |
| Ambassador | Invites new members using referral code |
| Node | Runs Pi Node software on a computer, contributing to consensus |

### The Mining Rate

The Pi mining rate started at 3.1416 Pi/hour (a nod to π) and has decreased as the network grows. This mimics Bitcoin's scarcity model — early miners earn more. The current base mining rate continues to adjust based on network size.

**Mining rate formula:** Base rate × Security Circle bonus × Node bonus

## Pi Milestones and Phases

Pi Network has progressed through several phases:

### Phase 1: Beta (2019)
Launch of the mobile app. Mining begins. Community grows rapidly through referrals.

### Phase 2: Testnet (2020–2021)
Pi's blockchain is tested. Nodes come online. KYC (Know Your Customer) verification begins to ensure real humans in the network.

### Phase 3: Open Mainnet (2022–Present)
Pi's blockchain goes live. Verified users can migrate their Pi to the main blockchain. Pi becomes transferable within the Pi ecosystem. Real-world utility apps emerge in the Pi App Platform.

### Phase 4: Open Network (Future)
Pi becomes freely tradeable on external exchanges, opening up price discovery and full liquidity.

## KYC Verification — Why It Matters

KYC verification is essential for your Pi to be migrated to mainnet. Pi Network requires KYC to:
- Ensure only real humans hold Pi (combating bot farms)
- Comply with global financial regulations
- Establish a trustworthy user base for the ecosystem
