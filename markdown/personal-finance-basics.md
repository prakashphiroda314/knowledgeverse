Most people were never taught how money works. Schools teach algebra but not compound interest. Parents pass on habits, not strategies. This guide changes that — giving you the financial foundation that changes everything.

## The Wealth Formula (It's Simpler Than You Think)

Wealth = (Income − Expenses) × Time × Returns

That's it. Every personal finance strategy is a variation on increasing income, reducing expenses, extending time, or maximizing returns. Understanding this formula is the first step to financial freedom.

:::info
**The most important variable is time.** Starting at 25 versus 35 can literally double your retirement wealth due to compound interest. Start now, even if it's small.
:::

## The Financial Order of Operations

Follow this sequence — in order — to optimize your financial decisions:

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Emergency fund (3–6 months expenses) | Avoid debt when life happens |
| 2 | Employer 401k match (if available) | 100% instant return |
| 3 | Pay high-interest debt (>7%) | Guaranteed 20% return if credit cards |
| 4 | Max Roth IRA ($7,000/year in 2024) | Tax-free growth for life |
| 5 | Max 401k ($23,000/year in 2024) | Tax-deferred growth |
| 6 | Invest in taxable brokerage | No limits, full flexibility |
| 7 | Build additional wealth | Real estate, business, etc. |

## Budgeting: The 50/30/20 Rule

A simple starting framework:

- **50%** — Needs (rent, food, utilities, insurance)
- **30%** — Wants (restaurants, entertainment, subscriptions)
- **20%** — Savings and debt repayment

This isn't dogma. High-income earners should save 40–60%. People in expensive cities may need 60% for needs. The point is intentionality.

## The Power of Compound Interest

Einstein reportedly called compound interest the eighth wonder of the world. Here's why:

**$500/month invested from age 25 to 65 (8% average annual return):**
- Total contributions: $240,000
- Final value: **$1,745,000**

**$500/month invested from age 35 to 65:**
- Total contributions: $180,000
- Final value: **$745,000**

Waiting 10 years cost you $1,000,000 in wealth — despite only contributing $60,000 less.

## Investing Basics: What You Need to Know

### Index Funds: The Default Answer

For most people, index funds are the optimal investment strategy. They provide:
- Instant diversification across thousands of companies
- Market-matching returns (historically 7–10% annually)
- Extremely low fees (0.03%–0.20%)
- No stock-picking required

Warren Buffett's advice to his trustee: "Put 90% in a low-cost S&P 500 index fund."

### Asset Allocation

Your allocation should reflect your time horizon and risk tolerance:

```
Aggressive (25-35 years old):
- 90% stocks (70% US, 20% International)
- 10% bonds

Moderate (35-50 years old):
- 70% stocks
- 30% bonds

Conservative (50+ years old):
- 50% stocks
- 50% bonds
```

### The Accounts That Matter

- **Roth IRA:** Contribute after-tax money. Grows tax-free. Withdrawals in retirement tax-free. Best for young earners.
- **Traditional IRA/401k:** Contribute pre-tax. Reduces current taxable income. Pay taxes in retirement.
- **HSA (Health Savings Account):** Triple tax advantage — deductible contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses. The ultimate tax shelter.
- **529:** Tax-advantaged savings for education.

## Debt Strategy

Not all debt is equal. Prioritize by interest rate:

:::danger
**High-interest debt (credit cards, payday loans >7%):** Pay off immediately. A 24% credit card is guaranteed negative 24% return on your money. No investment beats this mathematically.
:::

**Low-interest debt (<4%, like mortgages or student loans):** May be worth carrying. The math often favors investing rather than early payoff when rates are low.

## Building Multiple Income Streams

True financial security comes from multiple income streams:
1. **Active income:** Your job/business
2. **Investment income:** Dividends, interest, capital gains
3. **Rental income:** Real estate
4. **Business income:** Side projects
5. **Royalty income:** Digital products, content, IP

You don't need all five. Even adding one additional income stream dramatically improves financial resilience.

## FAQ

### How much should I have saved by each age?

A common benchmark: save 1x your salary by 30, 3x by 40, 6x by 50, 8x by 60. These are guidelines, not gospel. More important is your personal retirement spending needs.

### Should I pay off my mortgage early or invest?

If your mortgage rate is <4%, mathematically you're usually better off investing the extra money (historical market returns ~7-10%). If debt causes anxiety, paying it off early has real psychological value — that matters too.

### Is crypto a good investment?

Crypto should be a small speculative portion of a diversified portfolio (<5-10%) for those who understand the volatility and technology. It's not a substitute for index fund investing.
